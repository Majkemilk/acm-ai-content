Introduction

In today's digital landscape, adding accessibility features like auto-generated captions and subtitles to videos is vital for reaching a broader audience. This article will guide you through the process of adding auto captions and subtitles to videos using VEED, a user-friendly online video editing platform. You'll learn about the prerequisites, decision-making rules, trade-offs, step-by-step workflow, and practical templates to streamline your video editing process.

What you need to know first

Before diving into adding captions with VEED, it's essential to understand a few key concepts:

  • Video editing basics: Familiarize yourself with uploading and editing video files.
  • Caption formats: Understand the different types of captions (e.g., hard-coded vs. soft-coded). VEED primarily focuses on soft-coded captions that can be turned on or off.
  • Accessibility importance: Captions improve engagement and accessibility for viewers who are deaf or hard of hearing.

Decision rules:

When to use this approach:

  • When targeting a diverse audience with varying language preferences.
  • When your video's content relies heavily on dialogue or spoken instructions.
  • If you need to comply with accessibility standards or regulations.

Tradeoffs:

Pros/Cons:

  • Pros:
    • Enhances viewer comprehension and engagement.
    • Improves SEO as search engines index captions.
    • Customizable font styles and sizes.
  • Cons:
    • Auto-generated captions may require manual adjustments for accuracy.
    • Time-consuming if substantial editing is needed post-generation.

Failure modes:

What can go wrong and how to avoid it:

  • Incorrect transcription due to unclear audio quality—ensure your audio is clear before generating captions.
  • Misalignment of captions with video content—review and adjust timing as necessary to ensure accuracy.
  • Inconsistency in appearance—test various styles within VEED to find a cohesive look that fits your brand.

SOP checklist:

Step-by-step checklist:

  1. Upload your video to VEED.
  2. Select the "Subtitles" option.
  3. Choose "Auto-generate subtitles".
  4. Review the auto-generated text and make necessary edits.
  5. Adjust the timing of the subtitles if needed.
  6. Style your captions (fonts, colors, sizes) to match your preferences.
  7. Export your video with captions included.
