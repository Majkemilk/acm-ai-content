Introduction

In today's digital marketing landscape, email remains a powerful tool for engaging with customers. Yet, the challenge lies in crafting subject lines that capture attention and drive open rates. This article will guide you on how to use AI effectively to improve your email subject lines. You'll learn key concepts, decision rules, and step-by-step workflows, along with templates and examples that can kickstart your email marketing efforts.

What you need to know first

Before diving into the techniques, it's crucial to understand several concepts. First, the relevance of subject lines: they are often the first impression recipients have of your email. Second, the importance of personalization—using the recipient's name or past purchase history can increase open rates. Finally, familiarity with AI tools that can help generate subject lines based on effective strategies is vital.

Decision rules:

When to use this approach:

  • If your current open rates are under 20%
  • When A/B testing new subject lines is needed
  • If your target audience is not responding to existing email campaigns

Tradeoffs:

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros: Increases open rates, saves time, allows testing of various headlines quickly.
  • Cons: May lack a personal touch, reliance on AI without human editing can lead to off-brand messaging.

Failure modes:

What can go wrong and how to avoid it:

  • Using irrelevant or too generic subject lines may cause emails to get ignored. Always tailor to your audience.
  • Overly promotional language can make emails appear spammy. Aim for authenticity and value.
  • Failing to A/B test subject lines means missing out on discovering what truly drives engagement. Always test before full deployment.

SOP checklist:

Step-by-step checklist:

  • Identify your target audience
  • Analyze previous email campaigns for performance
  • Choose an AI tool for subject line generation
  • Generate several options for subject lines
  • A/B test the top options
  • Review results and apply insights to future campaigns

When NOT to use this


Avoid relying solely on AI for crafting subject lines if your brand requires a very specific tone that AI might not replicate. Additionally, if you have a niche audience with unique preferences, human insight may be necessary to resonate effectively.
