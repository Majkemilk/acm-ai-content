Introduction

In today’s digital landscape, videos are a primary means of communication. However, accessibility can often be a concern, especially for viewers who are hard of hearing or speak different languages. This article will guide you on how to add auto-generated captions and subtitles to videos with VEED, providing step-by-step instructions to enhance your video content effectively.

What you need to know first

Before diving into the process, it's important to understand a few key concepts related to video editing and subtitle creation. Familiarity with video formats, audio clarity, and the importance of accurate transcription can significantly improve the quality of your captions.

Decision rules:

When to use this approach:

  • If your audience includes non-native speakers.
  • When you want to improve accessibility for viewers with hearing impairments.
  • For educational videos to assist comprehension.

Tradeoffs:

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros:
    • Enhances viewer accessibility.
    • Expands potential audience reach.
    • Improves engagement and viewer retention.
  • Cons:
    • Auto-generated captions may not always be perfectly accurate.
    • Time-consuming to edit and finalize captions.

Failure modes:

What can go wrong and how to avoid it:

  • Inaccurate transcriptions due to poor audio quality: ensure clear audio before uploading.
  • Faulty synchronization of captions: preview your video to check timing.
  • Failure to edit auto-generated text: always review and edit automatically generated captions.

SOP checklist:

Step-by-Step Checklist:

  1. Upload your video to VEED.
  2. Select the "Subtitles" option from the menu.
  3. Click on "Auto-Generate Subtitles."
  4. Choose the language for transcription.
  5. Review and edit the generated captions.
  6. Adjust the timing of the captions if necessary.
  7. Export your video with the added subtitles.

Step-by-step workflow

  1. Begin by accessing the VEED platform and signing in.
  2. Upload your chosen video file by clicking on "Upload."
  3. From the main editing menu, select the "Subtitles" tab.
  4. Choose the “Auto-Generate” option to start the captioning process.
  5. Select your desired language and allow VEED to process the video.
  6. Review the subtitles generated for accuracy.
  7. Edit any captions as necessary to ensure they reflect the spoken content.
  8. Adjust synchronization as needed to verify that all captions align with the dialogue.
  9. Once finalized, click on “Export” to render the video with the added subtitles.

When NOT to use this

Avoid using auto-generated captions in the following situations:

  • When your video contains highly technical jargon or specialized language that may not be accurately transcribed.
  • If the audio quality is poor or distorted, which can lead to misinterpretations.
  • In situations where precise timing is critical, such as live events or webinars.
