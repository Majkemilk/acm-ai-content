## Introduction

Small businesses often struggle with maximizing the value of their content while managing limited resources. An effective ai content repurposing workflow can enhance a small business's reach and engagement without significant additional effort. By leveraging AI tools, they can transform existing content into new formats, thereby improving efficiency and effectiveness in marketing. When this is NOT worth it: If the content requires significant reworking or if compliance issues are prevalent, it may not be a suitable solution.

## What you need to know first

- The quality and format of the original content are crucial; poorly produced content may yield unsatisfactory repurposed results.
- Ensure all legal and compliance considerations are addressed; this is especially critical for businesses in regulated industries.
- Understand the target audience for each repurposed format to avoid alienating segments of your customer base.
- The turnaround time for repurposed content may vary based on complexity, so timelines should be clearly defined.
- Approval processes can delay the workflow; ensure you have designated stakeholders ready to provide timely input.

## Main content

### Decision rules:

- If you have high-quality existing content, then consider repurposing it for new channels to maximize ROI.
- If you are limited on budget, then prioritize repurposing content with the greatest audience engagement.
- If you struggle with time constraints, then use AI tools to expedite content transformation.
- Avoid using repurposing when the original content requires extensive updates, as this can negate efficiency benefits.
- If your audience is primarily on visual platforms, then favor multimedia formats over text-heavy formats for repurposing.
- Avoid repurposing outdated content that no longer aligns with your brand message or audience needs because it can harm your brand credibility.

### Tradeoffs:

- Speed vs Quality: Rapid repurposing might compromise the quality of the content, affecting audience engagement.
- Volume vs Control: Increasing the number of repurposed pieces can dilute brand messaging and focus.
- Automation vs Personal Touch: Relying heavily on AI may result in content that lacks a personal connection, potentially disengaging audiences.

### Failure modes:

- Automated content can lack nuance, causing misinterpretation of the original message. To mitigate this, always review generated content before publishing.
- Inconsistent branding may occur if repurposed materials are not unified in style. Establish clear guidelines for all content formats to prevent this.
- Over-reliance on AI tools might result in failure to understand audience preferences. Regularly collect feedback to adjust your strategy.

### SOP checklist:

- Identify existing content to repurpose.
- Analyze the target audience for new formats.
- Select appropriate AI tools for content transformation.
- Generate new content and ensure it meets quality standards.
- Review and approve the repurposed content.
- Publish the content across chosen channels.
- Measure engagement and gather feedback for future improvement.

## Step-by-step workflow (practical)

1. Identify high-performing content pieces that can be repurposed.
2. Analyze the audience for potential new formats.
3. Choose suitable AI tools like Descript or Pictory for the repurposing task.
4. Generate a new format (e.g., infographic, video) from the original content.
5. Review generated content to ensure it meets quality and compliance standards.
6. Get approvals from relevant stakeholders.
7. Publish the newly transformed content across chosen platforms.

**Inputs / Outputs**

- Inputs: Original content, audience data, chosen AI tools.
- Outputs: Repurposed content pieces in various formats.

**Common pitfalls**

- Failing to define the target audience for the new format can lead to decreased engagement.
- Neglecting to provide a thorough review of the repurposed content may result in miscommunication of the original message.
- Overloading channels with too much repurposed content can lead to audience fatigue.

## When NOT to use this

- Avoid repurposing content that is outdated, as it may not resonate with current audience needs.
- Avoid using AI tools if original content is of low quality, as this can perpetuate shortcomings in new formats.
- Avoid repurposing when internal resources for approval and review are unavailable, as this can lead to delays and quality issues.
- Avoid using AI tools for content requiring heavy personalization or nuanced understanding of the audience because automated responses may miss key context.

## FAQ

1. **What types of content can I repurpose?**  
   You can repurpose blog posts, videos, podcasts, and social media content into formats like infographics, ebooks, or new articles. Ensure the original content is high-quality for better results.

2. **How do I measure the success of repurposed content?**  
   Assess metrics such as engagement rates, shares, and traffic increases to your website or social media platforms. Use tools like Google Analytics for tracking.

3. **What if my repurposed content doesn't perform well?**  
   Review audience feedback and engagement metrics. Consider updating the repurposed piece or choosing different formats based on audience preferences.

4. **Can I automate the entire repurposing process?**  
   While AI tools can streamline many aspects, don't fully rely on them. Human oversight is essential to maintain quality and relevance.

5. **How often should I repurpose old content?**  
   Aim to repurpose content regularly, but not so frequently that it overwhelms your audience. A good practice is to repurpose a piece of content every few weeks based on engagement metrics.
