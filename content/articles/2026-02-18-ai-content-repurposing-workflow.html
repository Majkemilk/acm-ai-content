Introduction

In today's fast-paced digital landscape, small businesses often struggle to create fresh and engaging content regularly. This article will guide you through an effective AI content repurposing workflow that allows you to maximize the value of your existing content. You’ll learn how to transform old materials into new formats, saving both time and resources while increasing your online visibility.

What you need to know first

Before diving into the workflow, it's essential to understand some key concepts:

  • Content Repurposing: The practice of taking existing content and adapting it into different formats to reach a broader audience.
  • AI Tools: Various AI-powered platforms can assist in content repurposing by automating tasks such as transcription, video editing, and content generation.
  • Target Audience: Understanding your audience is critical to tailoring repurposed content that meets their needs and preferences.

Decision rules:

When to use this approach:

  • When you have existing content that is still valuable but has become underutilized.
  • When you are looking to diversify your content formats to engage different segments of your audience.
  • When you need to improve your SEO and visibility but lack the resources to produce entirely new content.

Tradeoffs:

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros:
    • Maximizes ROI on existing content.
    • Increases content reach without needing to create new content from scratch.
    • Enhances brand visibility by targeting different platforms and audiences.
  • Cons:
    • Risk of audience fatigue if the same content is repurposed too frequently.
    • Quality may decrease if not repurposed thoughtfully.
    • Requires an understanding of various formats to be effective.

Failure modes:

What can go wrong and how to avoid it:

  • Over-repetition: Avoid repurposing the same content too often to prevent audience disengagement.
  • Poor Quality: Always ensure that repurposed content maintains high standards. Use AI tools judiciously to enhance, not diminish, quality.
  • Neglecting Platform Differences: Tailor content for each platform rather than doing a one-size-fits-all repurpose.

SOP checklist:

Step-by-step checklist:

  1. Identify existing content that can be repurposed.
  2. Choose the new format (e.g., blog post, video, graphic).
  3. Outline how the content will be adapted for the new format.
  4. Utilize AI tools to assist in transforming the content.
  5. Review and edit to ensure quality.
  6. Distribute the repurposed content across chosen platforms.
  7. Monitor performance and gather feedback for future improvements.

Step-by-step workflow

  1. Human → Prompt #1 (to a general AI) → Prompt #2 (for the specific tool, e.g., Descript) → Use in the tool.
  2. Gather analytics on existing content performance.
  3. Identify specific content pieces with high engagement.
  4. Select the most appropriate AI tools for each content format.
  5. Use AI to automate tasks like transcription, editing, and formatting.
  6. Publish the new content format and distribute through various channels.

Goal: Transform a blog post into an engaging video script.
Task: Please create a prompt that will analyze a blog post and produce a video script that includes an introduction, main points, and a conclusion.
Uncertainty Flagging: If you are unsure about any element, please state so and ask for clarification before proceeding.
Permission to ask clarifying questions: If context is insufficient, please ask for more details before generating the script.

When NOT to use this

Avoid using this approach when:

  • Your existing content is outdated or irrelevant.
  • The target audience has shifted significantly since the original content was created.
  • Your brand message has fundamentally changed.

FAQ

  • What is content repurposing? It’s the process of adapting existing content into new formats, thereby reaching different audience segments.
  • How does AI help in this process? AI tools can automate tedious tasks like transcription, video editing, and creating content outlines, making the process more efficient.
  • Is there a risk of losing quality? Yes, but by following best practices and ensuring thoughtful repurposing, you can maintain or even enhance quality.
