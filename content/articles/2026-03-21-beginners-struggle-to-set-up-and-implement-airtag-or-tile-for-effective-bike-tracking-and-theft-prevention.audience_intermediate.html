Introduction

If you're looking to secure your bike against theft, you might have noticed the buzz around AirTag and Tile. Both options promise to help you locate your bicycle if it goes missing. However, beginners can struggle with setting them up and getting a strong option out of these devices. In this guide, we’ll go through what you need to know to choose between AirTag and Tile, along with some practical tips for effective bike tracking.

What to look for when choosing bike tracking devices

When selecting a tracking device for your bike, consider the following factors:

  • Range: Different devices have varying connectivity ranges. Make sure the one you choose covers your area.
  • Battery Life: Look for devices that have long-lasting batteries. You don’t want a device conking out on you when you need it the most.
  • Durability: Given that your bike will face different weather conditions, opt for waterproof options.
  • Compatibility: Ensure the device syncs easily with your smartphone or other apps.
  • Price: Everyone has a budget. Comparisons can help find the right fit without breaking the bank.

List of products

Here's a comparison of some popular options for bike tracking:

Apple AirTag

A user-friendly option that integrates seamlessly with Apple devices.

  • Pros: Easy setup, great range, highly reliable.
  • Cons: capped at Apple users, battery replacement needed after a year.

Tile Pro

The Tile Pro is versatile and works with a variety of smartphones.

  • Pros: Works on both iOS and Android, supports community find feature.
  • Cons: Battery lasts only a year, somewhat smaller range compared to AirTag.

Tile Mate

A budget-friendly option that still offers good tracking capabilities.

  • Pros: Affordable, simple to use, decent range.
  • Cons: Shorter battery life, lacks premium features of Tile Pro.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can I use AirTag with an Android device? No, AirTag is designed for Apple devices only.

2. How long do the batteries last? Tile devices typically last about one year, while AirTag batteries last about a year before needing to be replaced.

3. Is tracking real-time? Both devices offer location updates, but Tile requires a connection to other users’ devices for real-time tracking.
