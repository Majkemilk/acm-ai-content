AI Content Repurposing Workflow

Introduction

The AI content repurposing workflow is essential for small business marketing leads looking to maximize their content’s lifespan and reach. By efficiently transforming existing materials into various formats, businesses can engage different audience segments and drive more traffic. This approach allows for optimized utilization of time and resources, helping to maintain a consistent presence across multiple platforms. However, it may not be worth pursuing if the original content quality is low or if there are strict compliance requirements involved in content creation.

What you need to know first

  • These workflows are most effective when there's a solid inventory of original content, such as articles, podcasts, or videos.
  • Depending on your team’s size and skills, the complexity of the repurposing process may require additional resources or tools.
  • Approval processes within your organization can slow down content transformation, impacting the timely execution of campaigns.
  • Ensure that the repurposed content complies with brand guidelines and legal standards relevant to your industry.

Decision rules:

  • If you have multiple formats of content (e.g., a blog, video, and infographic), then consider repurposing them to reach different audiences.
  • If the original content has proven engagement metrics, then prioritize its conversion into new formats instead of creating new content from scratch.
  • When the turnaround time is tight, then focus on simple repurposing techniques, like creating quote graphics from existing text content.
  • Avoid repurposing content that is outdated or irrelevant, because it might not resonate with your intended audience.
  • If your audience prefers shorter formats, then transform long-form content into bite-sized pieces rather than lengthy adaptations.
  • Do NOT use this workflow if your content frequently requires heavy approvals, as this could delay the repurposing process significantly.

Tradeoffs:

  • Repurposing may require more initial time investment vs. directly creating new content.
  • While repurposing can expand reach, it may dilute the original message if not done carefully.
  • Balancing quality vs. quantity can be challenging; focusing on too many formats may compromise the depth of the content.

Failure modes:

  • Failing to track performance metrics from repurposed content can result in wasted effort; set up clear KPIs to evaluate success.
  • If the content doesn’t align with current audience interests, it will not generate engagement; conduct audience research before repurposing.
  • Overlooking legal standards in repurposed content could lead to compliance issues; always confirm that new formats adhere to regulations.

SOP checklist:

  • Review existing content inventory for potential repurposing opportunities.
  • Define goals for each repurposed piece (e.g., increase engagement, drive website traffic).
  • Choose appropriate formats based on audience preferences and content type.
  • Draft new versions or adapt existing scripts for different formats.
  • Develop an approval workflow to ensure compliance and brand alignment.
  • Set performance metrics for tracking the success of repurposed content.
  • Schedule publication and promotion of the repurposed materials.

Step-by-step workflow (practical)

  1. Identify existing content that can be repurposed.
  2. Analyze audience engagement metrics to prioritize high-performing pieces.
  3. Decide on the new formats that align with your audience’s preferences.
  4. Create outlines or scripts for the new content formats.
  5. Assemble a team or allocate resources needed for adaptation.
  6. Implement the content through established workflows.
  7. Monitor performance and gather feedback to improve future repurposing.

**Inputs / Outputs**

  • Inputs: Original content, target audience data, performance metrics.
  • Outputs: Repurposed content in various formats, engagement analytics.

**Common pitfalls**

  • Overcomplicating the repurposing process, which can lead to delays.
  • Neglecting to engage with the audience's current preferences, resulting in low interaction.
  • Skipping the performance analysis phase, leading to repeated mistakes in content strategy.

When NOT to use this

  • Avoid when content is highly technical or regulated, because it can lead to compliance issues if transformed improperly.
  • Avoid when your team lacks the skills required for effective adaptation, as this may result in subpar content.
  • Avoid when rapid component turnaround is not feasible, as this could hinder overall marketing timelines.

FAQ

  1. What should I do if the original content isn’t generating results?
  • Review content metrics to understand the target audience's needs, and consider repositioning the content before repurposing.
  1. How can I ensure that the repurposed content resonates with my audience?
  • Conduct audience research and gather feedback on previous content before beginning the repurposing workflow.
  1. What if I don't have enough resources to handle the repurposing?
  • Consider prioritizing fewer formats or adopting a phased approach, tackling one piece at a time depending on your team's capacity.
  1. What are good performance metrics to track for repurposed content?
  • Track engagement rates, click-through rates, and shares across platforms to measure success.
  1. Can I repurpose content from other brands?
  • It is advisable to avoid directly copying others' content unless you have explicit permission, to ensure compliance and respect copyright laws.
