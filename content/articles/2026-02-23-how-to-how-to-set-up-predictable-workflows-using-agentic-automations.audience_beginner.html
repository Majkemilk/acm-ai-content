Introduction

Setting up predictable workflows using agentic automations can significantly improve efficiency and save time in your work processes. In this guide, we'll cover the basics of agentic automation, decision rules to consider, tradeoffs involved, common failure modes, and step-by-step workflows. You'll also find ready-to-use templates and practical tips to help you get started.

What you need to know first

Before diving into agentic automations, it's essential to understand what they are and how they work. Agentic automation involves the use of AI-based tools that can perform tasks based on defined rules and triggers without requiring constant human intervention.

  • Familiarize yourself with the concept of workflows, which are step-by-step processes that outline how tasks flow from one person or tool to another.
  • Understand key automation tools available in the market that can facilitate agentic workflows.
  • Learn about triggering events that initiate automation, such as changing a status or receiving an email.

Decision rules:

When to use this approach:

  • When tasks are repetitive and require minimal human judgment.
  • If you need to streamline communication between multiple tools or platforms.
  • When you want to reduce the workload on human employees, allowing them to focus on more strategic tasks.

Tradeoffs:

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros:
    • Increased efficiency and productivity.
    • Reduction of human error in processes.
    • Ability to focus human efforts on high-value tasks.
  • Cons:
    • Initial setup may be time-consuming.
    • Requires ongoing maintenance and monitoring.
    • Potential challenges with integrating various tools.

Failure modes:

Common pitfalls and how to avoid them:

  • Inadequate testing of workflows before full-scale implementation can lead to errors.
  • Neglecting to update workflows as processes evolve may render them inefficient.
  • Over-automation can lead to loss of personal touch in customer interactions.

SOP checklist:

Step-by-step checklist:

  • Identify repetitive tasks and processes that could benefit from automation.
  • Select appropriate automation tools that fit your requirements.
  • Define clear workflows that outline each step of the process.
  • Test the automation in a controlled environment before full implementation.
  • Monitor the automated workflows regularly to ensure they function as intended.

Step-by-step workflow

  1. Identify the specific task or process you want to automate.
  2. Select the right automation tool that can perform the task efficiently (examples include Make or Descript).
  3. Design a clear outline of the workflow, detailing each step involved.

  5. Monitor the workflow for any issues and adjust as necessary.

### Lead-Follow-up Email Automation Prompt

1. **Define Lead Stages**:
   - Identify the stages of the lead journey (e.g., New Lead, Follow-Up, Qualified Lead, Lost Lead).
   - Document criteria for moving leads between stages.

2. **Configure Make**:
   - Log into your Make account.
   - Create a new scenario for lead tracking.

3. **Connect Lead Sources**:
   - Add HTTP/Webhooks module in Make to capture lead data from your website or landing page.
   - Connect CRM (like Salesforce or HubSpot) to Make for seamless data import.

4. **Set Up Triggers**:
   - Create a trigger for new leads entering the system.
   - Set conditions (e.g., specific content downloaded, form filled) to launch follow-up sequences.

5. **Design Email Templates in Descript**:
   - Open Descript and create personalized email templates for each lead stage.
   - Include dynamic fields (e.g., lead name, company) for personalization.

6. **Integrate Descript with Make**:
   - Use the Descript integration in Make to automatically fetch the desired email templates.

7. **Schedule Follow-Up Emails**:
   - In Make, add a series of email actions to send follow-ups based on time delays (e.g., 1 day, 3 days, 1 week).
   - Set up different emails for various lead stages (e.g., initial follow-up, query response).

8. **Add Conditional Logic**:
   - Use filters in Make to determine if a lead has responded or moved to the next stage.
   - If a lead responds, stop the follow-up sequence; if not, continue sending emails.

9. **Track and Optimize**:
   - Implement tracking to monitor open rates and responses.
   - Use analytics tools to gather data on follow-up effectiveness.

10. **Test the Workflow**:
    - Run the scenario in Make with test leads to ensure that all steps work as intended.
    - Double-check email formatting and dynamic field integration.

11. **Launch and Monitor**:
    - Activate the automation once testing is successful.
    - Regularly check the performance metrics and tweak the workflow as necessary for improvements.

### Ending Note
Make sure to refine your email content and follow-up timings based on user engagement for better results. If you need any clarification on specific steps or need further details, feel free to ask!

When NOT to use this

Avoid setting up agentic automations when processes require a high level of human judgment, creativity, or personal interaction. These scenarios could include brainstorming sessions, customer service situations requiring empathy, or any tasks where human nuance is essential.

FAQ

  • What tools can I use for agentic automation? There are multiple automation tools available, such as Make, Descript, and Zapier, that can help set up workflows effectively.
  • Can agentic automations run indefinitely? While they can run without human intervention, regular maintenance is necessary to ensure they function correctly over time.
  • Are there risks to automating my workflows? Yes, there's a risk of over-automation, which may lead to disconnect in communication or service quality with clients.
