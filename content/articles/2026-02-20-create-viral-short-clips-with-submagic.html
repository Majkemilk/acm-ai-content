Introduction

In today’s fast-paced digital landscape, creating viral short-form clips from long videos has emerged as a vital marketing strategy. This guide will walk you through how to leverage Submagic, an AI-powered editing tool, to streamline the process of transforming lengthy content into engaging snippets that can captivate audiences across various platforms.

What you need to know first

Before diving into creating viral clips, it's crucial to familiarize yourself with a few key concepts:

  • Short-form content: Generally, any video under two minutes, designed for quick consumption.
  • Engagement metrics: Understanding how to measure likes, shares, and comments can help you refine your video strategy.
  • Target audience: Knowing your viewers' preferences will guide the selection of clips that resonate with them.

Decision rules:

When to use this approach:

  • When you have lengthy content that can be distilled to highlight key messages.
  • If your goal is to boost engagement and reach a broader audience.
  • When utilizing platforms that favor short clips, like TikTok or Instagram Reels.

Tradeoffs:

Pros and Cons:

  • Pros:
    • Increases visibility and reach of your content.
    • Enhances viewer retention by delivering bite-sized information.
    • Boosts shareability across social media platforms.
  • Cons:
    • Some viewers may miss the context without the full-length video.
    • Requires continuous monitoring of metrics to optimize clip performance.

Failure modes:

What can go wrong and how to avoid it:

  • Clip too long: Keep clips concise; aim for under 60 seconds.
  • Ineffective content: Test different segments to see what resonates with your audience.
  • Lack of context: Provide necessary background through captions or voiceovers.

SOP checklist:

Step-by-step checklist:

  1. Identify key moments in your long video.
  2. Upload the video to Submagic.
  3. Utilize the AI features to select engaging clips automatically.
  4. Edit and annotate the clips for clarity and context.
  5. Export and optimize the clips for different social media platforms.
  6. Monitor engagement metrics post-publishing and iterate on your strategy.

When NOT to use this

Avoid creating short clips if your original content lacks engaging or substantial material. Additionally, bypass this method if you're targeting audiences that prefer in-depth information over snippets.
