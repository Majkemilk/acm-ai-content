Introduction

In today’s fast-paced digital world, a streamlined content approval process is crucial for maximizing productivity and minimizing delays. This guide will delve into how to utilize AI tools effectively to enhance and expedite your content approval workflows. By the end of this article, you will learn about essential tools, key concepts, and a practical step-by-step workflow to optimize approvals, thereby reducing bottlenecks.

What you need to know first

Before diving into AI tools, it’s important to understand the critical aspects of the content approval process. Typically, this process involves various stakeholders, including content creators, editors, and approvers, each playing a significant role. Familiarity with these roles, as well as common challenges like communication gaps and feedback loops, will help you leverage AI tools most effectively.

Decision rules:

  • If feedback is repetitive, consider using AI-based content drafting tools.
  • Use automation tools when managing multiple content streams for efficiency.
  • Refer to the Try it yourself section for easy implementation of workflows.

Tradeoffs:

  • AI tools can reduce manual workload but may require initial setup time.
  • There might be resistance from staff accustomed to traditional practices.
  • Over-reliance on AI can lead to a lack of personalized feedback.

Failure modes:

  • Neglecting training can result in poor tool utilization—ensure adequate training sessions.
  • Forgetting to monitor ongoing performance could lead to unnoticed inefficiencies.
  • Failure to integrate AI tools with existing systems may create data silos.

SOP checklist:

  • Identify content approval bottlenecks.
  • Test AI tools for drafting and feedback.
  • Provide training for team members on tool usage.
  • Integrate AI tools into the existing workflow.
  • Monitor tool effectiveness regularly.

Step-by-step workflow

  1. Identify key stakeholders involved in the content approval process.
  2. Map out the current approval workflow to identify inefficiencies.
  3. Select suitable AI tools based on identified bottlenecks.
  4. Human → Prompt #1 (to AI chat) → AI returns ready-to-use Prompt #2 or questions or instruction → Human (paste Prompt #2 into AI chat or follow the instructions given)
  5. Test the AI tool to create drafts and gather feedback.
  6. Implement the AI tool into the approval workflow.
  7. Regularly review and optimize the process based on performance metrics.

Inputs / Outputs

  • Inputs: Content briefs, stakeholder feedback, AI tool data.
  • Outputs: Drafted content, approval responses, task completion reports.

Common pitfalls

  • Pitfall: Lack of clear guidelines on using AI tools. Mitigation: Establish detailed user manuals.
  • Pitfall: Team members may resist adopting new technologies. Mitigation: Conduct training sessions highlighting benefits.
  • Pitfall: AI-generated content may lack creativity. Mitigation: Always involve human reviewers in the final stages.

### Prompt #2 for Make

---

**Title:** Automate Content Approval Process for Speedy Feedback

**Objective:** Streamline and automate the feedback collection and approval tracking for content created in Descript and Opus Clip.

**Instructions:**

1. **Set Up Triggers:**
   - Use Make to create a trigger for new content created in Descript or Opus Clip. This could be an upload of a new file or a completion of a draft.

2. **Create Feedback Collection Workflow:**
   - When new content is identified as ready for review, automate the following:
     - Send an email notification to all stakeholders with a link to the content.
     - Include a feedback form link (Google Forms, Typeform) for quick responses.
     - Set a deadline for feedback submission (e.g., 48 hours).

3. **Track Approval Status:**
   - As feedback is collected, set up an automated status tracker that captures:
     - Names of stakeholders who provided feedback.
     - Timestamps of feedback submission.
     - Any comments or approvals declined.
   - Consolidate responses into a Google Sheet or Airtable for easy tracking.

4. **Follow-Up Reminders:**
   - Automate follow-up emails (using Make) for stakeholders who haven’t provided feedback as the deadline approaches.

5. **Completion Notification:**
   - Once the feedback deadline has passed, automatically compile the feedback, categorize it into actionable items, and send a summary email to all stakeholders with next steps.

6. **Integration with Content Creation Tools:**
   - Integrate with Descript and Opus Clip to update content drafts based on the collected feedback as necessary.

---

**Expected Outcome:** By automating feedback collection and tracking approval statuses, aim to reduce approval timelines significantly and ensure clear communication with stakeholders throughout the process.

When NOT to use this

Avoid implementing AI tools during high-pressure periods or when the team lacks experience, as this can lead to confusion and decreased productivity. It’s also wise to refrain from automating critical decision-making processes that require a human touch.

FAQ

What types of AI tools are effective for content approval?
A variety of AI tools exist, including those for content generation, editing, and feedback management. Tools such as Descript and Make are commonly used.
Will AI tools completely replace human reviewers?
No, AI tools are designed to enhance rather than replace human creativity and oversight in the approval process.
How can I measure the effectiveness of AI tools in my workflow?
Monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) such as turnaround time for approvals, stakeholder satisfaction, and the volume of feedback received to evaluate effectiveness.
